About Us

We are a group of college students from St. Louis, MO devoted to providing students and our peers with access to safe information that has not been affected by political bias.

Personal Beliefs

Kevin Bacani: My name is Kevin Bacani and I am a freshman here at Saint Louis University in the flight science program of Parks College. I was drawn towards delving into the polarization of news and politics because I believe it is something that has great implications on American society without a lot of people realizing so. I hope to shed light on the issue and help inform someone on how this is affecting us, to what degree, and how to be a smarter user of social media and consumer of news/politics.

Logan Capps: I am a freshman studying Biomedical Engineering at Saint Louis University. Polarization in online news has really affected many relationships I have with other people including friends and family. It is difficult to have a comfortable discourse with people I know who are heavily involved in using online social media as a new source. My goal within Polarization Police is to create a better informed individual who has knowledge to understand both perspectives during political discussion.

Adam Walker: I am a freshman studying Aerospace Engineering at Saint Louis University. I believe that the polarization of the American public is mainly due to the use of social media to spread political news and ideals. I have seen debates on social media where people only become further entrenched in their ideals due to the use of biased and harmful language.
